My about page

  • Jun 2, 2025
  • 4 minute read

It’s come to my attention that the things that I had written on my last about page weren’t quite what I wanted to have there anymore. So, here I am, writing another one.

It’s kind of funny to me that I just put the “about” pages as regular posts and then link them on the “about” tab, but I guess it makes sense because I use these as regular status updates, and that’s really what a blog is for anyway.

Yesterday, I did another one of my 中文博客貼, which might be a little broken or not grammatically-correct, but that’s up for all the native speakers out there to judge (please give me feedback if you have any! I know I’m a super-beginner).

I know that this site is a little bit hard to search, so you might have to go by date if you want to see a post from any length of time ago.

Now, it’s time for my elevator pitch:

I’m a second year microelectronics/electronics engineering student who is also double-majoring in Mandarin, which I honestly get way more into most of the time, but after all, that’s why it’s in the degree, too.

I also play the clarinet, but I have most of my fun with that during the school year — now that the long summer term has started, I’m going to have to get my musical motivation back after some time. I probably should, though, because it is fun.

I know that I have a few friends who read my blog, and I think maybe some of my family members read it sometimes, too. The friends are who I feel like I’m writing to when I start putting a post down, though.

Recently, I heard a quote that I thought was pretty great and definitely applicable in a lot of cases (for myself and for people I know): that the “reasonable person adapts to the world, but the unreasonable person adapts the world to themselves”.

That’s all of the enabling that I need in order to go ahead and be as unreasonable as I want. I mean, I know I’m not the only one who’s gripes with society take up too much of my headspace. At the time of writing, I went to Taipei last week and I saw a society that has Mandarin, traditional characters, no 兒化, a super-clean subway, beautiful streets, stupid cute ads everywhere, really great food, and prices that are equally excellent. I really feel like everywhere across the world should be taking notes. Can we all have something like that?

I have another sort of a side-hobby, which is photography with my phone camera. In order to temper my expectations of quality and to give myself some challenge to breed creativity, I try to use only relatively bad cameras (mainly my phone camera, which is an iPhone SE 3, which honestly is pretty excellent and not much of a limiter). I post these photos on my blog frequently, and I also have a (somewhat secret and new) photo gallery page where this blog used to be, at this link.

I also have my mom’s old Canon handheld camera, which is fine but it’s a little too questionable for even my taste. Although, someday I’m still planning on buying an old Sony Mavica or something along those lines and having fun with it.

So, anyway, if you’re looking to learn more about me, here’s a checklist of the things I think are cool:

  1. Scallion pancakes and spicy falafel pockets
  2. bo po mo fo ㄅ ㄆ ㄇ ㄈ
  3. Dense and interesting cities
  4. Trains (below ground, above ground, slow, fast, medium speed)
  5. Landmarks that nobody else cares about
  6. 3:15 pm 3點1刻 brand coffee milk tea
  7. Matcha flavored 大白兔 White Rabbit
  8. Humble, unassuming desk setups

I appreciate the look into my blog. I really hope you enjoy. And if you’re already a reader (like one of my two friends), I hope you appreciate the general life-status update.

Have yourself a day!
